## 2026-07-03 — CORRECTION: the concurrency ceiling is the podman store lock, not CPU (transient_store is the fix)

The prior bed-concurrency note (2026.184.0836) was WRONG and is corrected here (R1
doc-divergence incident). It claimed "Podman is NOT the bottleneck", "transient_store
disproven", and "CPU load is the ONE ceiling" — all false. RCA of the aggressive-pool
failures (reading the ACTUAL error, not assuming) showed EVERY failure was `Error:
beginning transaction: database is locked`: podman's single sqlite container-store
write lock, contended past its busy-timeout under concurrent bed cycles. Proven: at
maxjobs 14 → 0 locks; at maxjobs 22 → every bed locks. **`transient_store = true`
(container run-state → per-boot tmpfs DB) is the fix** — same maxjobs 22 → 0
`database is locked`, 32/37 pass. CPU/RAM/disk have headroom at that concurrency. The
reap (orphan naming) is complementary hygiene, not the concurrency fix. Also records
the R1 lesson: two wrong hypotheses (bogus "CPU-oversaturation" load-gate + bogus
"cache-mount race" sharing=locked) each cost a cutover by ASSUMING instead of READING
the `database is locked` error. Documentation-only.
