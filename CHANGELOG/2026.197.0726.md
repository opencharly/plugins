whose `command:` steps shell out to bare `$PATH` `charly`) tripped the
stale-binary freshness guard. The teammate "fixed" it by running the (now
removed) system-wide install task — a HOST install of `/usr/bin/charly` from
an UNMERGED branch, violating the per-worktree-binary discipline the agents
skill's own 4e item had already proven (2026-07-12, spikes S0+S0b) but never
stated as a general rule with its own name.

Added ONE new authoritative section, "The charly binary in a multi-teammate /
multi-worktree setup", to `internals/skills/agents/SKILL.md` (the sole owner
of multi-agent discipline per the Authoritative-copy registry). It covers:
two binaries and their two roles; the host-install boundary; the
stale-binary guard's per-tree semantics (`charly/main_freshness.go`); why a
host-local bed can never gate worktree work; the multi-worktree concurrency
corollary (no freeze barrier needed ACROSS distinct worktrees, unlike the
shared-tree B3 model); a pointer to the side-effects already documented in
the go skill; and the post-merge resync procedure.

Added clarifying cross-notes (not restatements) at the THREE places that
already touched this territory, so the shared-tree freeze rule and the new

## Member 2 — rule 8: within-worktree self-freeze (the complementary rule)

Minutes after the first incident, a second incident proved the COMPLEMENT of
the same rule: a teammate edited a header comment in its own worktree while
its OWN `check-sidecar-pod` bed run was mid-flight, at step 6. The per-tree
freshness guard tripped on the bed's next step (`check-live`), citing "newer
source: `charly/materialize.go`", and failed an otherwise-green run.
Correctly RCA'd as self-inflicted (not a product defect), the teammate
rebuilt via `task build:binary` and re-ran fresh. Where the first incident
showed "across worktrees, no freeze is needed", this one showed the
complementary boundary: "within ONE worktree, freeze your OWN `charly/*.go`
while your OWN bed is mid-run" — the guard checks at EVERY heavy verb, not
just at the start.

Added a new bullet, "Within-worktree self-freeze — the COMPLEMENTARY rule",
to the same section: the two freeze scopes are complementary, not the same
rule, and neither replaces the other.

`charly`/`install`/`install-arch` Taskfile tasks, which built AND installed

## Member 5 — the PATH-prefix rule: `./bin/charly` alone is not enough

A THIRD live incident, proving a variant the section implied but never stated
explicitly: a teammate ran its bed as `./bin/charly check run <bed>`
(relative path, correct binary) but WITHOUT the `$PATH` prefix. A `command:`
step INSIDE the bed's OWN plan shelled out to bare `charly`, which resolved
to the host-installed binary; ITS freshness guard walked up from the
worktree cwd, saw the teammate's edited sources, and refused — failing ONE
step of an otherwise-green 22-step run, after 908 seconds. RCA: the OUTER
invocation's binary does NOT propagate to an INNER bare-`charly` subprocess a
plan step launches — only a `$PATH` prefix does.

Added a new bullet stating the rule explicitly, adjacent to "Host-local beds
are NEVER a worktree gate": always invoke a bed as `PATH=$PWD/bin:$PATH
./bin/charly check run <bed>` — the form "Per-worktree binaries" (4e) already
recommended, now cross-referenced and given a name — plus the failure
signature (a single stale-binary step failure deep inside an otherwise-green
run, often on a step testing an unrelated surface) so a future teammate
recognizes the symptom instead of re-deriving the RCA from scratch.
